# Chapter 7: Calm Before the Storm

## Prologue: The General's Diary

*Location: Tent of General Marcus, Valley of Kings*
*Time: Three Weeks After the Summit*

*The men are restless. We have been camped here for weeks, staring at the fog in the valley. The enemy does not show himself. It is a waiting game, and I hate waiting.*

*The 'Alliance' holds, barely. The Elves complain about the Orcs' hygiene. The Orcs complain about the Elves' singing. The Dwarves just drink and complain about everything.*

*But there is hope. The 'Heroes'—Kaelen and his motley crew—have become something of a legend in the camp. They train with the men. They eat with them. They are the glue.*

*I only fear that glue will melt when the fire starts.*

***

## Part 1: The Training Grounds

*Location: Alliance Forward Base*
*Time: Mid-Morning*

The clatter of wooden swords filled the air. Dust rose in choking clouds.

Kaelen stood in the center of the ring, shirtless, sweat glistening on his scars. He was facing three opponents at once: an Eldorian Knight, an Orc Berzerker, and an Elven bladedancer.

"Come on!" Kaelen taunted, spinning his training blades. "Is that all the Alliance has to offer?"

The Orc roared and charged. Kaelen sidestepped, tripping the massive warrior. The Knight swung a heavy overhead blow. Kaelen caught the wooden blade with his crossed swords, twisting and disarming him.

The Elf was faster. She darted in, aiming for his ribs.

*System Alert: Attack Incoming. Trajectory: Left Flank.*

Kaelen didn't look. He ducked, letting the blade pass inches over his head, and swept her legs.

Three down. In ten seconds.

"Dead. Dead. And... mostly dead," Kaelen pointed at the Orc, who was trying to get up.

Applause broke out from the soldiers watching on the fence.

"Show off," Lyra called out. She was sitting on the fence, peeling an orange. Her new 'Alliance' armor—sleek black leather with silver trim—fitted her perfectly.

"It's called morale, Lyra," Kaelen wiped his face with a towel. "They need to see that skill beats size."

"Easy for you to say, Mr. 'I-Can-Read-Attack-Patterns'," she hopped down. "The General wants you. War Council."

"Again?" Kaelen groaned. "We went over the supply lines yesterday. We have enough grain for a month."

"It's not supplies," Lyra's face went serious. "Scouts found something. Or rather... someone."

***

## Part 2: The Gilded Cage

Kaelen entered the Command Tent. The map table was crowded. Elfiria was there, looking regal but exhausted.

"Kaelen," she nodded. "We have a situation."

She pointed to the map. A section of the valley, near the old ruins.

"A patrol went missing here last night," General Marcus said. "We found them this morning. Flayed."

Kaelen stiffened. "Demons."

"Worse," Grok growled. "A message was carved into their chests. 'The King comes'."

"They are taunting us," Elfiria said. "Trying to provoke a premature attack."

"It's working," Marcus slammed the table. "My men want blood! We cannot sit here while they butcher our brothers!"

"If we march into the fog, we die," Kaelen said. "That's what they want. They have the terrain advantage."

"So we do nothing?" Marcus challenged.

"We wait for the Sword Saint," Kaelen said. "Aric is two days away. When he arrives, we have a spearhead. Until then... we hold."

The meeting adjourned in a foul mood.

Kaelen stayed behind with Elfiria.

"You look like you haven't slept in a week," Kaelen said gently.

"I dream of fire," she admitted, rubbing her temples. "Every time I close my eyes. Kaelen... I'm scared."

"Good," Kaelen said. "Fear keeps you sharp."

"I don't want to be sharp," she whispered. "I want to be... just Elfiria."

She looked at him. The tent was empty. The guards were outside.

"Take a walk with me," she said. "Please."

***

## Part 3: The Balcony of Stars

They walked to a cliff overlooking the camp. The sun was setting, painting the sky in violent hues of orange and purple. The wind carried the scent of pine and distant campfires.

"It's beautiful," Elfiria said, leaning against the railing. "If you ignore the army of monsters down there."

"Perspective is everything," Kaelen leaned against a tree, watching her. The dying light caught the silver in her hair, turning it into a halo.

"Kaelen," Elfiria turned to him. "If we lose..."

"We won't."

"If we do. I want you to know..." She stepped closer. The air between them shifted, charged with something heavier than war.

"I never wanted to be a leader," she confessed, her voice trembling. "I was the third child. I was supposed to study magic and eat lemon cakes. I wasn't supposed to lead armies."

"You're doing a good job," Kaelen said softly.

"Am I?" She looked down at her hands. "I feel like a fraud. Everyone looks at me and sees the High Priestess. The Symbol. But inside... I'm just a girl who doesn't want to die."

"That's why you're a good leader," Kaelen stepped closer, invading her personal space. "Because you know the cost. Marcus sees pieces on a board. You see people."

Elfiria looked up. Her violet eyes were wide, vulnerable. "And what do you see, Kaelen?"

"I see the woman who stood up to a council of wizards," Kaelen said, reaching out to tuck a strand of hair behind her ear. "I see the woman who walked into a war zone because it was right."

He let his hand linger on her cheek. Her skin was cool, soft as silk.

"I see Elfiria," he whispered.

Her breath hitched. She leaned into his touch, closing her eyes.

"Kaelen..."

"Elfiria."

She opened her eyes. The longing in them was raw, undisguised.

"Make me forget," she whispered. "Just for a moment. Make me forget the war."

Kaelen didn't hesitate. He pulled her to him.

Their lips met.

It wasn't the chaste, courtly kiss of a knight and a princess. It was the desperate, hungry kiss of two people standing on the edge of oblivion.

Elfiria made a small sound in her throat and wrapped her arms around his neck, pulling him down. Her fingers tangled in his hair, desperate to hold on. Kaelen's hands found her waist, pulling her flush against him. He could feel the rapid beat of her heart against his chest, matching his own.

The world fell away. There was no army. No demons. No duty. Just the warmth of her body, the taste of wine and honey on her lips, and the soft sighs she made as he deepened the kiss.

He walked her back, pressing her gently against the trunk of the ancient oak tree. She gasped as his lips moved to her jawline, then her neck.

"Kaelen," she breathed, her head falling back. "If we survive this..."

"When," he corrected, nipping lightly at the sensitive skin beneath her ear.

"When we survive this," she shivered. "Take me away. Anywhere. Just... away."

"I will," Kaelen promised. He kissed her again, slower this time, savoring the moment.

For a long time, there was only the sound of the wind and their ragged breathing.

Finally, they broke apart. Elfiria's cheeks were flushed a deep violet. Her lips were swollen. She looked... alive.

"Better?" Kaelen asked, resting his forehead against hers.

"Much," she smiled, a genuine, dazzling smile.

"Hey! Lovebirds!"

The voice cut through the air like a serrated knife.

They jumped apart.

Lyra was standing on the path, arms crossed. Her expression was a mask of indifference, but Kaelen saw the tightness in her jaw.

"Dinner bell," Lyra said flatly. "Drakon ate the first boar. If you want the second one, hurry up."

Elfiria quickly smoothed her robes and placed her circlet back on her head. The mask of the High Priestess slid back into place, but her eyes remained soft.

"Thank you, Lyra," Elfiria said, her voice steady. "We are coming."

She hurried back towards the camp.

Kaelen stayed for a second. He looked at Lyra.

"Lyra..."

"Don't," she turned away, kicking a stone. "Just don't. Let's go eat."

"You're jealous," Kaelen said, surprised.

Lyra froze. She turned back, her eyes blazing. "Jealous? No. I'm pragmatic. She's a Princess, Kaelen. You're a merc. It's a cliché. And it ends with you dead or heartbroken. Usually both."

"It's not like that."

"Isn't it?" She stepped closer, poking him in the chest. "Just... be careful. She has a kingdom to save. You're just... her guard. Don't forget which side of the tracks you live on."

She turned and marched away.

Kaelen watched her go, feeling a pang of guilt.

## Part 4: The Banquet of Fools

The mood at the banquet was surprisingly festive. The soldiers, unaware of the grim report, were celebrating the arrival of fresh ale supplies.

Drakon was the center of attention. He was arm-wrestling an Ogre.

"Hnnng!" The Ogre strained, veins bulging.

"Is that all?" Drakon yawned. *SLAM.* He pinned the Ogre's arm.

"Winner!" the crowd cheered.

"Who is next?" Drakon roared, holding up a tankard. "I take all challengers! Even you, tiny Elf!" He pointed at Lyra.

"Pass," Lyra said, poking at her stew. She was sitting next to Kaelen, but she felt miles away.

"About before..." Kaelen started.

"Eat your stew, hero," she muttered.

Suddenly, the ground shook.

*BOOM.*

A massive explosion tore through the perimeter wall.

The music stopped. The laughter died.

"Attack!" a sentry screamed. "They're inside!"

The tent flap was ripped open. But it wasn't a soldier.

It was a Demon. A massive, hulking brute with red skin and black iron armor. Malakor.

"Did I miss the toast?" Malakor sneered. His voice was like grinding rocks.

Behind him, hundreds of lesser demons poured into the camp.

"To arms!" Marcus shouted, drawing his sword.

Malakor laughed. He raised a hand, and a wave of Hellfire swept through the tent, incinerating the tables and the soldiers near the entrance.

"Chaos," Malakor breathed, inhaling the smoke. "Delicious."

Kaelen drew his swords. The calm was over. The Storm had broken.
