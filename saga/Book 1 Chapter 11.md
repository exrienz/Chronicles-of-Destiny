# Chapter 11: The Fall

## Prologue: The Feast

*Location: The Battlefield*
*Time: Nightfall*

The crows were feasting. But they were not alone.

Ghouls picked through the corpses, stripping armor and flesh. The moan of the dying filled the air—a symphony of suffering.

Malakor walked through the carnage. He found Aric's body.

The Saint's armor was twisted. His face was pale, eyes staring sightlessly at the dark sky. Nearby lay the shards of *Dawnbringer*.

"A waste," Malakor grunted. He kicked the body.

He picked up the hilt of the broken sword. It still hummed faintly with holy energy.

"A trophy for the King," Malakor mused. He hooked it to his belt.

He looked east, towards the fleeing remnants of the army.

"Hunt them down," he ordered his captains. "Leave no one alive."

***

## Part 1: The Canyon of Echoes

*Location: East of the Valley*
*Time: Midnight*

Kaelen didn't feel the reins in his hands. He didn't feel the horse thundering beneath him. He was numb.

"Faster!" Lyra yelled from her own horse. "They're gaining!"

Behind them, a pack of Shadow Knights on nightmare steeds were closing the distance. Their mounts didn't tire.

"We can't outrun them," Drakon shouted. He was running on foot—no horse could carry him—but his stamina was failing.

"The canyon!" Kaelen pointed. "The Screaming Pass! It's narrow!"

They veered off the main road, heading into the jagged rocks.

The pass was a winding labyrinth of stone. The wind howled through it, sounding like screams.

"Drakon! Block the path!"

"I am on it!"

Drakon stopped, turned, and punched a rock formation. The cliff face crumbled, creating a landslide that sealed the narrow path behind them.

They heard the Shadow Knights slam into the rocks on the other side. Frustrated screams echoed.

"That will hold them for an hour," Drakon panted, leaning against the wall.

Kaelen slid off his horse. His legs gave way, and he hit the dirt.

"Kaelen!" Elfiria crawled to him. She was pale, her eyes red.

"I'm fine," Kaelen lied. He looked at the group.

Lyra was bandaging a cut on her arm. Drakon was bleeding from his side. Elfiria was spiritually shattered.

"We failed," Elfiria whispered. "Everyone is dead."

"Not everyone," Kaelen said hoarsely. "We're here."

"For how long?" Lyra asked bitterly. "We have no food. No water. And an army is hunting us."

"We keep moving," Kaelen said. "Deeper into the canyon. There are caves."

***

## Part 2: The Darkness Within

They walked for hours. The canyon walls rose higher, blocking out the stars. It was pitch black.

"I cannot see," Elfiria said, stumbling.

"Hold my tail," Drakon offered gently.

They found a cave entrance. It was small, hidden behind a thorn bush.

"Inside," Kaelen said.

They squeezed in. The air was cool and damp.

They collapsed on the sandy floor.

"So," Lyra said into the darkness. "This is how it ends. Starving in a hole."

"We aren't dead yet," Kaelen said.

"Aric is dead!" Elfiria snapped. "The Sword Saint! If he couldn't stop them, what can we do? I'm just a useless Princess! And you're just a mercenary!"

Silence stretched.

"You're right," Kaelen said quietly. "We are nobodies. But nobodies survive. Heroes die."

"That's not comforting," Lyra muttered.

"It's not meant to be," Kaelen stood up. "I'm going to scout."

"I'll come with you," Lyra said.

"No. Stay with Elfiria."

Kaelen walked deeper into the cave. He needed to be alone. He needed to scream.

He found a small underground pool. He knelt and splashed water on his face. It was freezing.

He looked at his reflection in the water. He looked tired. Broken.

"System," he whispered. "Status."

"Quest failed," Kaelen laughed bitterly. "No kidding."

He sat back against the rock wall, closing his eyes.

A soft footstep approached.

"Kaelen?"

It was Elfiria. She sat beside him. She was shivering.

"Go back to the fire," Kaelen said.

"There is no fire," she said. "Drakon is asleep. Lyra is watching the entrance."

She hugged her knees. "I'm sorry. For what I said."

"You were telling the truth."

"No," she shook her head. "You're not just a mercenary. You're the only reason I'm breathing."

She looked at him. In the dim bioluminescence of the cave moss, her eyes shone.

"Hold me," she whispered. "Please. I'm so cold."

Kaelen opened his arms. She curled into him, burying her face in his chest. He wrapped his cloak around both of them.

"We're going to die here, aren't we?" she asked.

"No," Kaelen stroked her hair. "We'll find a way."

"If we do die," she looked up at him. "I don't want to die afraid. And I don't want to die... wondering."

"Wondering what?"

She didn't answer with words. She kissed him. It was a desperate, tear-stained kiss.

Kaelen held her tight. In the darkness, surrounded by stone and defeat, they found a small, fragile comfort in each other. It wasn't about lust. It was about affirmation. *I am here. You are here. We are alive.*

From the shadows of the tunnel entrance, Lyra watched for a moment. She didn't interrupt. She turned and walked back to her post, her hand resting on the hilt of her dagger.

***

## Part 3: The Discovery

Drakon woke them an hour later.

"Kaelen," the dragon whispered. "The pool. Look."

Kaelen disentangled himself from Elfiria and looked at the water.

A faint blue light was coming from the bottom.

He leaned closer. It wasn't a reflection. It was a glow coming from deep underwater.

"It's magical," Elfiria said, rubbing sleep from her eyes. "But not mana. It feels... mechanical."

"Mechanical?" Lyra asked, joining them.

"Old One tech," Drakon rumbled. "My grandfather spoke of it. The ancients who built the world."

"There's a tunnel underwater," Kaelen pointed. "I can see the opening."

"We have to swim?" Lyra shivered. "I hate swimming."

"It's better than waiting for the demons to find us," Kaelen said.

He took a deep breath. "I'll go first."

He dove. The water was icy. He swam down towards the blue light. He squeezed through a narrow opening in the rock.

He surfaced on the other side.

He gasped.

He was in a massive cavern. But it wasn't natural. The walls were smooth metal. Strange, glowing lines pulsed on the ceiling. In the center was a platform.

"It's a Vault," Kaelen whispered.

He swam back.

"It's clear," he told the others. "You have to see it."

They swam through. When Elfiria surfaced and saw the cavern, she started crying again. But this time, it was awe.

"The myths are true," she whispered. "The Sanctuary of the Stars."

They climbed onto the platform. It was dry and warm.

"Safe," Drakon laid down on the metal floor. "It feels safe."

"The demons won't find us here," Kaelen said. "The rock blocks their sensing magic."

They had found a place to hide. A place to heal.

But as Kaelen looked at the alien machinery, he wondered if they had found something else.

Something that could fight back.
